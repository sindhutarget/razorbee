THE COLD COLOUR & LIGHT SHOW

  • March 9, 2020 March 18, 2020
  • admin
  • Uncategorized

The cold colour,the sound & light show & the visitor…

This is a story that has been there for long within & I thought of sharing it, read on..

Laces tightened,socks pulled up,back pack fastened & all other gear in place looking up at the clear sky and the sun shone bright.. looking at the towering mountain lacing the skyline yonder, I smiled & said to myself..will be closer there this evening. I had to cover a good 12 kilometres to pitch tent.

A prayer in the mind & hope filling me, I started my trek with very beautiful vistas around me with the sound of the gushing waters from a stream beside me fed by a glacier from above made the start a stellar one. Barely a kilometre or so into the trek, I stopped in my tracks as I was facing a chasm.. I knew the journey had just begun..

Not looking back, I started my descent into the chasm, which was crumbling on the walls as I set foot on the loose gravel. Slowly, steadily & very carefully making my way down, I reached down & looked up the other side where I had to ascend with the same conditions.In a zig zag fashion managed to climb using my hands & kicking into the gravel to create a foothold. The chasm was easily 15 to 20 feet deep & around 30 feet wide. That was exhaustive. With a breather for a little while, I continued on my chosen path..my love for the mountains!!!

Treading on the flat surface for a few kilometres with a brief stopover for a quick bite of the bread with boiled eggs gone frozen because the wind chill had started setting in. By now the time was around 13.30 hrs, I knew the clock was ticking for me. To block the wind, wearing another layer & face mask on I set out only to be faced with an steep ascent on a rocky surface turning me a mountain goat.. I went ballistic scaling the rocky surface, which I enjoyed very much whilst taking a toll on me at the same time & testing my endurance to the hilt.

As I scaled this terrain..”Dark Grey..The Cold Colour” was enthusiastically engulfing the sky in front of me.. I had very little time left on hand to reach my destination for the day which I saw yonder at the start of the trek. The time now was 15.30 hrs, I was now looking at the very wide stream flowing rapidly in front of me which I had to cross & yes the water was freezing. Quickly taking off my shoes tying it to the back pack stepped into the freezing water with rocks & stones below, the freezing water emptied & numbed my sensesüòÖüòÇ

Putting this wonderful experience behind me with a smile, I moved in the direction heading to my final destination of the day which was a few kilometres away. By now the skies were threatening with even more enthusiasm to let loose on earth. By now the wind chill had set in completely with even more furore making it difficult to walk.The feet & shoulders had started to hurt as they were stiff by now, Though very weary I had to cover the little distance left & that is when the drizzle started caressing my face as if this wasn’t enough it turned into a downpour with the rumbling skies the entire region around me illuminated as the lightning flashed across the sky. With streaks of lightning across the sky in front of me was a sight to behold., I can visualise it even now, coming to think of it. Then the inevitable happened..”The Hailstorm..it rained hailstones like marbles falling out of the box, hitting me hard”

By now I had reached my site..quickly pitching tent, took refuge from the storm outside. The time now was around close to 18.00 hrs. Lighting up the butane cylinder made myself a cup of Chai to ease my nerves. By now it was dark in the middle of nowhere & the storm outside had eased. Suprisingly the sky cleared the stars shown brightly.I was at approximately 12000 ft. Getting down to brass tacks, I made myself little comfortable for a while & made Maggi noodles for myself..phew that gave me some solace as I felt warm.

Little did I know what was coming my way..around 22.00 hrs as I was tucked into my sleeping bag, I heard this deafening cracking sound that echoed frightfully & dangerously for a while making even the earth under me rumble. I froze at that moment not knowing what had happened, I was very uneasy within. that was a dirty feeling. As I was falling asleep,around midnight the sound of the utensils being moved around scarily took my breath away.. that is when I had a “Visitor” It was a snow leopard!!!!!! I was clueless as to what I had to do, In case..

Unable to fall asleep, I finally stepped out of my tent to take in some fresh air sitting on the rock and as I looked up to the skies.
.Lo & Behold..I saw the Milky way.. what a beautiful sight that was. Sitting there for a little while in the great outdoor braving the freezing temperatures looking back into the day earlier on, I told myself what an eventful day it was.. All of this in one day..Wow!!!!!!

By the way..the following morning I saw that a part of the glacier had torn away opposite my tent that made the deafening cracking noise the previous night!!!

Yes, that is when I told myself..The Cold Colour..The Sound & Light Show & The Visitor..now when I look back to that day..I look at it in awe sending a flurry of joy, peace & calm!!!!!

The snow is sparkling like a million little suns

The very cute Marmots, inhabitants of the Himalayan region are a part of the squirrel family & a very shy lot. They dive deep into their burrows upon seeing “Humans & Predators”
My first tryst with these chaps goes back a long way! On my first visit to the Ladakh region & on my way to Chang La pass, I briefly stopped to soak in the beauty & breathe in some fresh air whilst looking at a few wild horses running in the distance & a herd of wild yaks grazing. Little did I realise I was being watched!!!!!!

I realised this when I eventually noticed some movement in the vicinity with a very distinctive call. Upon probing I saw lots of burrows neatly dug into the earth & this prompted me to retract in my footsteps & as I was doing this, I noticed a liitle fellow looking at me from the mouth of his abode, cautiously & steadily he made his way out looking at me & the very sight of him melted me & brought a huge smile onto my face, the chap was so adorable & lovable!!!

But me being the wildlife aficionado chose to see it from a distance not disturbing it in its natural habitat where..

“I Was The Intruder”

Also I did not feed it any anything as their food comprises of blooms from bushes, plants grass & occasionally insects. Spending some time there watching these beautiful creatures was an absolute delight & blowing a kiss to these tiny, cute & beautiful beings going about their daily lives I left for my destination wearing a huge smile üòÑüòÑ

Its not any person I am shaming but its their behaviour! How irresponsible can one get?! For their share of fun they drink & break these bottles out there in the very beautiful & pristine surroundings leaving these poor creatures at immense risk of hurting themselves leaving them to bleed to death???? Or in this case these creatures nibbling on the human toxic waste!!!

Humans……you are not destroying nature or its creatures, you are destroying yourself!!!

Whoever you are…shame on you for littering!!!!üò°üò°üò°
